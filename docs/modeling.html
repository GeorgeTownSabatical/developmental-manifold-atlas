Modeling

Formal Principle

No hypothesis may be treated as meaningful unless it survives correction for body size, sex, age, measurement quality, and, where relevant, phylogeny.

Level 1

Descriptive morphometrics: allometry, ratios, residuals, missingness, quality maps.

Level 2

Comparative developmental models: regression, mixed effects, causal diagrams, phylogenetic correction.

Level 3

Manifold and dynamical models: diffusion maps, trajectory inference, Bayesian state-space models, TDA, sheaf integration.

Null and Sensitivity Models

Registered tests should compare against body-size-only, sex/age-only, phylogeny-only, measurement-protocol-only, missingness-only, and permutation null models before publication claims are promoted.
